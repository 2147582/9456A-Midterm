Guitar General Description

A Guitar is an instrument that usually has six strings. It is played with two hands by either strumming or plucking the string to make different types of sounds. The left hand will manipulate the strings by pressing against the frets. The right hand will control the sound of the strings being produced.

In this tutorial we will be using an acoustic guitar, an Acoustic guitar is made out of wood. An acoustic has nine parts the head stock, body, tuning key, neck, nut, bridge, soundhole, string, and fretboard. Each of the mentioned parts play a vital role in the guitar.

String Composition

The standard string composition is E, A, D, G, B, and E, where the first E is the 6th string and A is the 5th. Usually the index finger is placed on the strings at the first fret that is nearest to the head stock followed by the middle finger, ring finger and so on.

Parts of a Guitar

Head Stock
The headstock as the name says is the head of the guitar. The headstock's main function is to hold the strings at the head of the guitar. The headstock contains the tuning key and the nut.
Tuning Key
The tuning key hold the strings of the guitar. Rotating the tuning key allows the control of the pitch in each string. Tuning a guitar by just listening to each strings' sound is a hard skill to master specially for beginners. If you tighten the strings too much it will easily be broken.
Neck
The neck holds the headstock, strings, and the fretboard. The bottom part of the neck is connected in the guitar. It is mostly occupied by the fretboard.
Nut
The nut has narrow vertical space in its surface. These narrow spaces keep your strings in place. It is placed at the top part of the guitar.
Bridge
The bridge is the one that holds the strings at the other end. It contains string pegs where the string is attached.
Soundhole
The soundhole provides the acoustics of the guitar. Strumming or plucking the strings over the soundhole amplifies the sound and makes it clearer.
Strings
The string is the one that makes different sounds by pressing strings in different manners on the fretboard.
Fret Board
Oftenly mistaken as the neck, the fret board acts as a guide to where the fingers must be placed in order to create a chord. It is the wooden section on the guitar after the headstock where the strings pass by. The tune of each corresponding strings get higher when the fingers are pressed on the fret board that are closer to the sound hole.
